Hypothesis

We believe that having separate queues for benefits means that:

  • people can see when they have tasks to do that they need someone else to do (because they don't have access)
  • if a benefit system isn't working due to technical issues people can carry on working on tasks for other benefits
  • people can do all tasks for one benefit as a group, they don't have to switch systems

We believe that providing a search by NINO function allows agents to view historic records for management checks or find the information needed to answer enquiries

We believe that providing an edit option for the response on a historic record allows agents to update a task from not updated to updated or handed off when they have more information

We believe that having a overall count of the team tasks to be completed allows everyone to see when there is a period of high fluctuation and more people might be needed to help out

We believe that highlighting if there are tasks that approach 3 days - it will help people prioritise those benefits first

We believe that if I want to 'pause' a task (for example pass to team leader to do a bank validation check) the task will be 'closed' and the NINO passed to the team leader - the team leader can then pick up the task through a NINO search and update the response on the customer history page

We believe that they might come up with their own ways to split tasks fairly between themselves and keep track of how many each person has done (e.g. tally on paper)
